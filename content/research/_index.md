### Modelling the Socioeconomic Impacts of Zero Carbon Housing in Europe
**European Climate Foundation** · March 2022

With 40% of Europe's energy being used by buildings — translating to 36% of all CO₂ emissions — decarbonising the building stock is non-negotiable for reaching net-zero. This project analyses how Europe may look under different net-zero transition pathways in the heating sector, exploring scenarios from hydrogen boilers to heat pumps and their socio-economic implications.

*Authors: Finn-Henrik Barton, Stijn Van Hummelen, Jon Stenning, Pim Vercoulen*

[Technical Report](https://europeanclimate.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/modelling-the-socioeconomic-impact-of-zero-carbon-housing-in-europe-final-technical-report-march2022.pdf) · [Summary](https://europeanclimate.org/resources/renovating-and-and-electrifying-buildings-strengthens-europes-economy-and-energy-security/) · [Presentation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_fyCDXG8CuY)

### Macroeconomics of the Energy Union (Tasks 6 & 7)
**European Commission** · November 2022

Regional and sectoral implications of the clean energy transition, modelled using E3ME and GEM-E3-FIT. Task 6 analyses how different regions of Europe will be affected by the energy transition given their economic structure and natural resource endowments. Task 7 brings together all prior tasks to model a range of transition scenarios.

I contributed GIS-based data visualisations of regional and sectoral modelling outputs.

*Authors: Eva Alexandri, Sachin Babbar, Finn-Henrik Barton, Alice Brooke, Unnada Chewpreecha, and others*

[All Reports](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/data-and-analysis/energy-modelling/macroeconomic-modelling_en) · [Task 6](https://op.europa.eu/en/publication-detail/-/publication/f88dae3c-6953-11ed-b14f-01aa75ed71a1/language-en) · [Task 7](https://op.europa.eu/en/publication-detail/-/publication/962a54d1-6950-11ed-b14f-01aa75ed71a1/language-en)

### Renewable Energy Market Analysis: Africa and its Regions
**IRENA** · January 2022

Comprehensive analysis of renewable energy opportunities and macroeconomic impacts across Africa and its sub-regions. In conjunction with this project, IRENA contracted Cambridge Econometrics to expand E3ME to provide more granular economic modelling for Africa. I spent several months processing, verifying, and collating data for 12 new African regions plus the UAE to be added to the model.

*Authors: Ha Bui, Jamie Pirie, Hector Pollitt, Unnada Chewpreecha, An Vu, Zsófi Kőműves, Sophie Heald, Alistair Smith, Michael McGovern, Finn-Henrik Barton*

[Full Report](https://irena.org/-/media/Files/IRENA/Agency/Publication/2022/Jan/IRENA_Market_Africa_2022.pdf) · [Summary](https://irena.org/-/media/Files/IRENA/Agency/Publication/2022/Jan/IRENA_Market_Africa_2022_Summary.pdf)

### Warmer Homes and Lower Bills — The Impact of the Renovation Wave
**Rockwool Foundation** · October 2021

Assesses the potential impact of the EU Renovation Wave on gas demand for heating. The analysis was based on a new building stock model developed by Pim Vercoulen and myself. I conducted the scenario modelling used to calculate changes in consumer expenditures. Prepared in the lead-up to COP26.

*Authors: Jon Stenning, Ciaran Nevin, Finn-Henrik Barton, Ornella Dellaccio*

[Report](https://www.camecon.com/what/our-work/the-renovation-wave-can-cut-eu-gas-imports-and-reduce-consumer-bills/)

### Exploring the Shadow Cost Gradient of Parking Standards: A Geographically-Differentiated Approach
**Journal Article** · August 2020

Uses a Mixed Geographically Weighted Regression (MGWR) approach with a hedonic specification to estimate the cost of minimum parking requirements (MPRs) on commercial properties in Los Angeles County. I contributed GIS-based visualisations (Figures 2–5).

*Authors: Sofia F. Franco, W. Bowman Cutter, W. Skyler Lewis (supporting role: Finn-Henrik Barton — GIS visualisations)*

[PDF](https://scholarship.claremont.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1014&context=pomona_fac_econ)
